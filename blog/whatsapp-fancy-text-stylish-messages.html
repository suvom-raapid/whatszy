WhatsApp Fancy Text: How to Send Stylish Messages

Tutorial February 25, 2026 4 min read

Ever seen someone send a WhatsApp message in a completely different font? Maybe it looked like elegant handwriting, or the letters were circled, or the text appeared bolder than usual. They are not using a secret app or a hidden WhatsApp setting. They are using Unicode characters that look like different fonts.

The trick is simple once you know how it works, and it takes about ten seconds to do. Let's break it down.

What Is Fancy Text?

Unicode is the standard system that defines every character your phone and computer can display. Within that system, there are thousands of characters that look like stylized versions of the regular alphabet. A mathematical bold "A" (𝗔) is a separate character from the plain letter A, even though they represent the same thing to a human reader.

Fancy text generators take advantage of this. When you type "Hello" and convert it to bold Unicode, the tool replaces each letter with its mathematical bold equivalent. The result looks like a different font, but it is actually a string of entirely different characters. That is why it works everywhere, not just in WhatsApp. There is no special font file involved. The characters themselves carry the style.

Popular Styles You Can Use

Here are some of the most popular Unicode text styles. Each one is its own set of characters that renders across nearly all modern devices.

Bold: 𝗛𝗲𝗹𝗹𝗼 𝗪𝗼𝗿𝗹𝗱
Italic: 𝘏𝘦𝘭𝘭𝘰 𝘞𝘰𝘳𝘭𝘥
Bold Italic: 𝙃𝙚𝙡𝙡𝙤 𝙒𝙤𝙧𝙡𝙙
Script: 𝒽𝑒𝓁𝓁𝑜 𝓌𝑜𝓇𝓁𝒹
Double-struck: 𝕙𝕖𝕝𝕝𝕠 𝕨𝕠𝕣𝕝𝕕
Circled: ⓗⓔⓛⓛⓞ ⓦⓞⓡⓛⓓ
Squared: 🅷🅴🅻🅻🅾 🆆🅾🆁🅻🅳

Script gives your text an elegant, handwritten feel that works beautifully in birthday wishes or congratulatory messages. Double-struck has a mathematical quality that makes it stand out without being too flashy. Circled and squared text grabs attention immediately, which makes them popular for announcements and group chat headers.

How to Generate Fancy Text

You do not need to memorize any Unicode tables. A fancy text generator handles the conversion for you in seconds. Here is the process:

  • Step 1: Type your message in plain text. Keep it relatively short for best results.
  • Step 2: Choose a style from the available options. Most generators show you a live preview of every style at once, so you can compare them instantly.
  • Step 3: Copy the converted text. A single tap or click is usually all it takes.
  • Step 4: Open WhatsApp, paste the text into your message, and send. Your recipient will see the stylish text immediately.

Tips for Using Fancy Text

Fancy text is eye-catching, but a little goes a long way. Here are some practical tips to get the most out of it without annoying the people you are messaging.

  • Use it sparingly. A full paragraph in circled text is hard to read. Reserve fancy styles for short headers, names, or a key phrase you want to highlight.
  • Check older devices. Most modern phones render Unicode text perfectly, but very old devices or some budget models may display empty boxes or question marks instead. If you are unsure, stick to bold or italic Unicode since those have the widest support.
  • Great for group headers. If you manage a WhatsApp group, a fancy text title in the group description makes it look polished and professional.
  • Perfect for status updates. Your WhatsApp status stands out more when the text itself looks unique. Script and double-struck styles work particularly well here.
  • Combine with native formatting. You can use WhatsApp's built-in bold (*text*) alongside Unicode text. Check our guide to bold, italic, and strikethrough for details on native formatting.

Where Else Does Fancy Text Work?

Because fancy text is made up of standard Unicode characters, it is not limited to WhatsApp. The same styled text works in virtually any app or platform that supports text input.

  • Instagram — bios, captions, and comments
  • Twitter / X — tweets and profile names
  • Facebook — posts, comments, and page bios
  • Telegram — messages and channel descriptions
  • TikTok — bios and video captions
  • Email subject lines — though use this with caution, as some email clients may not render them well

The portability is one of the biggest advantages of Unicode text over platform-specific formatting. Generate it once, use it everywhere.
