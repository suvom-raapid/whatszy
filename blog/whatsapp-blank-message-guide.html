How to Send a Blank Message on WhatsApp

How-To 3 min read February 25, 2026

Sending a completely blank message on WhatsApp sounds impossible. After all, the send button stays grayed out unless there is something in the text field. You can tap it a hundred times and nothing happens. But there is actually a clever workaround that relies on special Unicode characters, and it takes just seconds to pull off.

Why Send a Blank Message?

It might seem pointless at first, but blank messages have a surprising number of uses. Here are a few popular ones:

  • Pranks and fun: Send an empty bubble to confuse your friends. Watch them try to figure out what happened.
  • Creating visual space in group chats: If an important message keeps getting buried, blank messages can push older content up and create breathing room.
  • Silent pings: Want to get someone's attention without actually saying anything? A blank message triggers a notification but delivers no content.
  • Artistic spacing in statuses: Use invisible characters to create clean, spaced-out text statuses that look polished and intentional.

The Unicode Trick

The secret behind blank messages is Unicode. Unicode is the standard that defines every character your phone can display, from regular letters to emojis to thousands of special symbols. Among those symbols are several invisible characters that take up space in a text field but render as nothing on screen.

The two most commonly used invisible characters are:

U+3164 — Hangul Filler (appears completely blank)
U+200E — Left-to-Right Mark (zero-width invisible character)

WhatsApp treats these characters as valid text input. That means the send button activates, the message goes through, and the recipient sees an empty chat bubble. It is not a glitch or a hack. It is simply a character that has no visible glyph.

Does It Work in Groups?

Yes. Blank messages work everywhere on WhatsApp: one-on-one DMs, group chats, and even text statuses. The behavior is consistent across Android, iOS, WhatsApp Web, and the desktop app. One small caveat: on some very old phone models or outdated WhatsApp versions, the recipient might see a tiny dot or a small square instead of a truly empty bubble. On modern devices, though, it appears completely blank.
